Welcome! I am currently a senior student at UCLA majoring in Applied Mathematics with Specialization in Computing. My research interests lie in the area of Optimization, Calculus of Variations, and Numerical Methods with applications in Machine Learning.

I am interested in pursuing a Ph.D in Applied Mathematics. My academic work has been uploaded to the UCLA Computational and Applied Mathematics [Archive](https://ww3.math.ucla.edu/camreports/) and [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Xinghao-Dong-3/research). This research with [Professor Koffi Enakoutsa](https://www.math.ucla.edu/people/visiting/koffi) on the topic of [***Morrey's Conjecture***](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/364956861_Some_Numerical_Simulations_in_Favor_of_the_Morrey's_Conjecture) in the context of Calculus of Variations deals with two properties of functions known as quasi-convexity and rank-one convexity. The report we composed not only details the procedures and results of our numerical approximations and optimization algorithms, but more importantly, it also introduces prominent previous efforts by experts and discussed the connections between Morrey’s conjecture and complex analysis, harmonic analysis, geometric function theory,probability, martingales, differential inclusions and planar non-linear elasticity.

I am taking my first graduate level math course [MATH 273A](https://sa.ucla.edu/ro/Public/SOC/Results/ClassDetail?term_cd=22F&subj_area_cd=MATH%20%20%20&crs_catlg_no=0273A%20%20%20&class_id=662382200&class_no=%20001%20%20) by [Professor Guido Montúfar](https://www.math.ucla.edu/~montufar/) in which I learn optimization theories and their applications in Machine Learning. I gave a presentation on the proof of Baillon-Haddad Theorem which shows the equivalency between co-coercive condition and L-lipschitz differentiability of a convex function. The presentation was highly acclaimed by the professor and classmates.

I also have teaching experience in both Mathematics and Computer Science when I served as the Learning Assistant for [MATH 32B](https://sa.ucla.edu/ro/Public/SOC/Results/ClassDetail?term_cd=22W&subj_area_cd=MATH%20%20%20&crs_catlg_no=0032B%20%20%20&class_id=262211210&class_no=%20002%20%20) and [CS 31](https://sa.ucla.edu/ro/Public/SOC/Results/ClassDetail?term_cd=221&subj_area_cd=COM%20SCI&crs_catlg_no=0031%20%20%20%20&class_id=187093910&class_no=%20001%20%20) at UCLA. My main duties were assisting professors and teaching assistants to answer questions, organize discussions and hold review sessions.
